About Me

Sam completed his undergraduate education at the University of Arizona, obtaining a Bachelors of Science (B.S.) degree in Chemical Engineering in the Spring of 2019.

Work

Sam is currently an Associate Research Engineer in Dow's Hydrocarbons R&D organization. In particular, Sam's group is researching propane dehydrogenation chemistry in the context of fluidized circulating bed processes. The specific technology that Sam supports is known as Fluid Catalytical Dehydrogenation (FCDh), a newer propane dehydrogenation technology which has the ability to be retrofitted into existing commercial hydrocarbon cracking units. In addition, this technology is competitive with other mainstream fluidized dehydrogenation processes (i.e UOP, Exxon) in terms of overall energy usage and dehydrogenation performance.

In this role, Sam has been involved in many stages of the research cycle: experiment design, material procurement, scheduling, data analysis, documentation and write-up of study results. Additional responsibilities have included the utilization of Good Engineering Practices (GEP), Management of Change (MOC), and adherance to mechanical integrity standards in plant operations and improvement projects.
